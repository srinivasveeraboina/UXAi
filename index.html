The Process

AI Across the UX Workflow

From the first user interview to the final A/B test — AI is a co-pilot at every step.

01 · Discover

Research & Insights

Run sentiment analysis on app store reviews, cluster interview transcripts, and auto-generate user personas grounded in real data.

  • Transcript clustering & affinity mapping
  • Sentiment & emotion detection
  • Auto-generated persona drafts
02 · Define

Problem Framing

Synthesize research into JTBD statements, user journey maps, and prioritized opportunity backlogs — in minutes.

  • Journey-map generation
  • Jobs-to-be-done extraction
  • Opportunity scoring
03 · Ideate

Concept Generation

Prompt AI to generate dozens of layout ideas, copy variants, and visual moodboards — perfect raw material for divergent thinking.

  • Layout & wireframe variants
  • Microcopy suggestions
  • Moodboard & style exploration
04 · Prototype

Build & Iterate

Turn sketches into clickable prototypes, auto-generate components, and keep design tokens consistent across a system.

  • Sketch-to-Figma conversion
  • Auto-component generation
  • Design-token sync
05 · Test

Validate Designs

Predict click maps, simulate user behavior, and run AI-moderated unmoderated tests at a fraction of the cost.

  • Attention & heatmap prediction
  • Synthetic user testing
  • Auto-summarized test sessions
06 · Deliver

Ship & Learn

Hand off pixel-perfect specs, auto-generate documentation, and feed live analytics back into the next design iteration.

  • Auto-generated specs & docs
  • Real-time experience monitoring
  • Continuous learning loops

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Thinking about adopting AI in your design practice? Here are the honest answers.

Will AI replace UX designers? +

No. AI accelerates execution but it cannot replace human empathy, strategic thinking, or contextual judgement. Designers who pair AI with strong fundamentals become dramatically more productive — the role evolves, it doesn't disappear.

Where should a team start with AI for UX? +

Start small — automate one painful task first. Most teams pick research synthesis or microcopy generation. Once you trust the output, expand into prototyping, accessibility checks, and design-system automation.

Is AI-generated design "good enough" to ship? +

Raw AI output is rarely ship-ready. Think of it as a fast first draft. Designers still curate, refine, validate with users, and apply brand judgement. The combination ships faster *and* better than either alone.

What about user privacy when using AI tools? +

Critical concern. Anonymize research data, prefer tools with enterprise data agreements, and never paste personally-identifiable information into public models. Privacy and ethics are now part of the designer's job.

Does AI introduce bias into UX design? +

Yes — AI inherits bias from its training data. Designers must actively audit outputs, test with diverse users, and use AI to *augment* inclusive practice, not replace it. Inclusive design remains a human responsibility.
